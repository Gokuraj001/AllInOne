Unit-2: General Methodology of Dance
Unit Overview:
This unit explores the foundational methodologies employed in the teaching and
learning of dance. It emphasizes the balance between technical mastery, creativity, and
cultural context to produce holistic dancers and choreographers. Students will critically
engage with various methods to develop personal artistry, historical awareness, and
pedagogical insight.
Unit Objectives:
By the end of this unit, students will be able to:
1. Understand and apply technical training methods to enhance movement
precision.
2. Explore improvisational techniques to cultivate creativity and self-expression.
3. Examine historical and cultural frameworks that influence dance styles and
practices.
4. Analyze and compare different dance pedagogies.

5. Apply learned methodologies through structured and free-form movement tasks.
6. Reflect on case studies to contextualize dance practice in real-world settings.
UNIT STRUCTURE:
1. Technical Training and Mastery of Movement
Topics Covered:
Importance of physical conditioning and alignment.
Techniques for body awareness, balance, flexibility, and coordination.
Structured technique classes (Ballet, Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Contemporary, etc.).
Floor work, barre work, center work.
Repetition and muscle memory in dance.

Key Methods:
Vaganova technique (Ballet)
Kalakshetra Bani (Bharatanatyam)
Cunningham technique (Contemporary)
Learning Activities:
Daily technique class.
Peer feedback sessions.
Video analysis of master performers.
Case Study:
Martha Graham’s approach to technique — how contraction and release revolutionized
modern dance and required new training regimens.

2. Improvisation and Creative Exploration
Topics Covered:
Definition and scope of improvisation in dance.
Tools for spontaneous movement: imagery, breath, space, and rhythm.
Structured improvisation vs. free improvisation.
Contact improvisation and group dynamics.
Role of improvisation in choreography and composition.
Learning Activities:
Improvisation jams.
Solo and group explorations using prompts (e.g., emotions, objects, poetry).
Movement diary and reflection logs.

Case Study:
Steve Paxton and the evolution of Contact Improvisation — analysis of its methodology
and influence on postmodern dance.
3. Historical and Cultural Contexts
Topics Covered:
Importance of context in understanding dance.
Ritual vs. performance dance.
Colonial and postcolonial influences on Indian classical dance.
Globalization and its impact on traditional dance forms.
Ethnographic approaches to studying dance.
Learning Activities:

Research and present a traditional dance form from a specific culture.
Comparative analysis of classical and contemporary versions of a dance.
Guest lecture by a traditional dance practitioner.
Case Study:
Rukmini Devi Arundale and the revival of Bharatanatyam — contextualizing the dance
in a cultural-nationalist framework.
Hip-Hop’s evolution from street culture to academic study — analyzing socio-political
context in dance expression.
4. Dance Pedagogy and Methodological Approaches
Topics Covered:
Pedagogical theories in dance: somatic, constructivist, experiential.
Dance as a discipline vs. dance as expression.

The role of the teacher/facilitator.
Safe dance practice and injury prevention.
Assessment in dance training.
Learning Activities:
Design a sample dance lesson plan.
Conduct peer-teaching sessions.
Review dance education policies (e.g., CBSE, NCERT frameworks for India).
Case Study:
Deborah Jowitt’s approach to dance criticism and education — linking practice with
critical pedagogy.

5. Integrative Practice and Method Development
Topics Covered:
Synthesizing technique, improvisation, and cultural knowledge.
Building personal movement language.
Choreographic research methods.
Documentation and archiving of dance.
Learning Activities:
Create a short solo or group performance integrating unit components.
Maintain a reflective portfolio with sketches, notes, and feedback.
Video documentation and peer-review.
Case Study:
Akram Khan’s fusion of Kathak and Contemporary — understanding how technical
mastery, improvisation, and cultural context coexist in his work.
